# 📯 Rally

## Overview

A **rally** is a coordinated alliance attack started by one player and joined by others.

Rallies allow multiple alliance members to combine troops against a common target.

Some strategic objectives are not meant to be taken by one player alone. A rally has a formation phase, a launch moment, and a locked combat journey after launch.

## 🎯 Purpose

Rallies are especially important for:

* attacking the Palace
* future alliance objectives
* large shared targets

## ⚙️ How a Rally Works

A rally follows a simple flow:

* one player starts the rally
* a formation timer begins
* alliance members can join before launch
* the rally launches automatically when the timer ends
* after launch, the troop composition is locked until the action is resolved

## 👥 Rally Participation

A rally is designed for alliance coordination.

During the formation phase, alliance members may join and prepare their troop contribution.

Once the rally has launched:

* new players can no longer join
* participants can no longer leave manually
* the troop composition stays locked until the rally is finished

## ⚔️ Why Rallies Matter

Rallies are one of the main ways alliances handle large-scale objectives.

They allow players to:

* combine strength against powerful targets
* coordinate attacks with other alliance members
* take part in strategic content that requires teamwork

## ⛔ Important Rule

A rally can only be changed while it is still forming.

After launch, the rally becomes a single coordinated action and must stay stable until it resolves.

## 💡 Tips for Players

### Join on time

If you want to participate, make sure to join before the rally launches.

### Watch your troop availability

Troops committed to a rally cannot be used in other actions until they are released or the rally is over.

### Follow alliance coordination

Rallies are most effective when players respond quickly and follow the planned target.
