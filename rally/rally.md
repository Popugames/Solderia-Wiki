# 📯 Rally

## Overview
A **rally** is a coordinated alliance attack started by one player and joined by other alliance members.

Rallies are used when a target is too important or too strong to be handled by one player alone.

---

## 🎯 Purpose
Rallies allow alliance members to combine troops into one organized attack.

They are especially important for:

- attacking the **Palace**
- contesting major alliance objectives
- coordinating stronger attacks against shared targets

---

## ⭐ Why Rallies Matter
Some objectives are designed for alliance play, not solo attacks.

A rally gives your alliance a way to:

- gather strength before launch
- attack with better coordination
- fight for strategic control
- support alliance-wide war plans

---

## ⚙️ How a Rally Works
A rally happens in three main steps:

### 1. 🕒 Formation Phase
A player with permission starts the rally and selects a target.

During this phase:

- alliance members can join
- players choose which troops to send
- the rally timer counts down to launch

### 2. 🚶 Launch
When the timer ends, the rally begins moving toward the target.

After launch:

- no new players can join
- players can no longer leave manually
- troop composition becomes locked

### 3. ⚔️ Battle and Resolution
Once the rally reaches the target, combat is resolved.

After the battle ends, the rally finishes normally and the troops return according to the result.

---

## 📌 Important Rule
A launched rally is treated as **one coordinated action**.

Because of that, the rally must remain stable after launch and cannot be freely changed.

---

## 💡 Tips for Players

### Join before the timer ends
If you want to participate, make sure to send troops during the formation phase.

### Check your troops first
Troops committed to a rally are no longer available for other actions.

### Follow alliance timing
Strong rallies depend on coordination. Watch alliance chat and rally timers carefully.
