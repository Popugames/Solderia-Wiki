# 📜 Rally Rules and Restrictions

## Overview
Rally actions follow strict rules to keep alliance warfare fair, clear, and stable.

These rules determine when players can join, leave, cancel, or interact with a rally.

---

## ✅ What Is Allowed
Players may:

- create a rally if they have permission
- join a rally during the formation phase
- leave a rally during the formation phase
- cancel a rally before launch if they are allowed to do so

---

## ⛔ What Is Not Allowed
Players may not:

- join a rally after it launches
- leave a rally after it launches
- cancel a rally after it launches
- use troops already committed to another action
- leave the alliance freely while they lead or participate in a launched rally if that would break the rally state

---

## ⚠️ Special Cases
If a player tries to leave the alliance or is removed while still tied to an active rally, the alliance change may be delayed until the rally is fully resolved.

This prevents invalid troop states and protects the rally from being broken mid-action.

---

## 🧭 Why These Restrictions Exist
These rules help prevent:

- broken rally states
- unfair last-second changes
- troop duplication or invalid troop use
- alliance abuse during strategic actions

---

## 💡 Tips for Players

### Plan before joining
Make sure you want to commit those troops before entering the rally.

### Watch the rally timer
Most player actions are only possible before launch.

### Avoid conflicts with other actions
Troops already used elsewhere will not be available for rally participation.
