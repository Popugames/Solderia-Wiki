# 🤝 Alliance

## Overview

An alliance allows players to work together, grow faster, coordinate attacks, and compete for major kingdom objectives.

Joining an alliance unlocks access to group features that are not available to solo players, including rallies, alliance communication, and large-scale strategic play.

---

## 🌟 Why Alliances Matter

Being in an alliance gives you several important advantages:

- access to coordinated attacks through **Rally**
- participation in major kingdom objectives such as the **Palace**
- support from other players through alliance help and cooperation
- easier progression through teamwork and shared activity
- access to alliance communication and leadership structure

Players who stay active in a strong alliance usually progress more efficiently than players who stay alone.

---

## 🧩 Basic Structure

An alliance normally includes:

- a **Leader**
- **Officers**
- **Members**
- permissions based on rank

This structure helps alliances stay organized during wars, events, and daily activity.

---

## ⚔️ What Alliances Allow You To Do

Alliance members can usually:

- communicate through alliance chat
- participate in rallies
- support alliance goals
- defend important objectives
- contest major kingdom structures such as the Palace

Some of the most important endgame systems in Solderia cannot be used effectively without an alliance.

---

## 🚀 Why Joining Early Matters

Players in active alliances often gain major advantages early on:

- stronger coordination
- access to rally content
- better support during wars and events
- more chances to take part in major strategic objectives

A smaller active alliance is often better than a large inactive one.

---

## 💡 Tips for Players

### Choose an active alliance
A coordinated alliance gives much more value than one that only exists on paper.

### Understand your role
Even regular members are important during rallies, Palace wars, and alliance objectives.

### Stay involved
Alliance content is designed around teamwork, not solo play.
