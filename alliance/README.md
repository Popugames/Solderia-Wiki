# 🤝 Alliance

## Overview
An Alliance allows players to work together, grow faster, coordinate attacks, and compete for major kingdom objectives.

Joining an alliance unlocks access to group features that are not available to solo players, including rally participation, alliance communication, and large-scale strategic play.

---

## Why Alliances Matter

Being in an alliance gives you several important advantages:

- access to coordinated attacks through **Rally**
- participation in major kingdom objectives such as the **Palace**
- support from other players through alliance help and cooperation
- easier progression through teamwork and shared activity
- access to alliance communication and leadership structure

Players who stay active in a strong alliance will usually progress more efficiently than players who stay alone.

---

## Alliance Roles

Alliances use a rank system to organize leadership and permissions.

### Leader
The Leader manages the alliance and has full control over major decisions.

A Leader can usually:

- edit alliance settings
- accept or reject applications
- promote or demote members
- remove members
- send alliance mail
- mark important targets
- start rally attacks
- transfer leadership
- disband the alliance

### Officers
Officers help manage the alliance and support the Leader.

An Officer can usually:

- accept or reject applications
- start rally attacks
- send alliance mail
- mark targets
- remove lower-ranked members

### Members
Members are standard alliance players.

A Member can usually:

- use alliance chat
- join rally attacks
- support alliance activity
- leave the alliance

Some alliances may use extra internal ranks, but the basic structure is always meant to separate leadership from regular members.

---

## Creating an Alliance

To create an alliance, a player must meet the required conditions.

Typical requirements include:

- reaching a minimum Castle level
- not already being in another alliance
- paying the alliance creation cost
- choosing a valid alliance name and tag

Alliance names and tags must be unique.

---

## Joining an Alliance

You can join an alliance in one of two ways:

### Open Join
If the alliance is open, you can join instantly.

### Apply to Join
If the alliance requires approval, you must send an application and wait for a Leader or Officer to accept it.

### Join Restrictions
You cannot join an alliance if:

- you are already in another alliance
- you are on alliance join cooldown
- you do not meet the alliance’s entry conditions

### Join Cooldown
After leaving an alliance or being removed, you may need to wait before joining another one.

This cooldown exists to prevent abuse during alliance wars and strategic events.

---

## Leaving an Alliance

You can leave an alliance, but not while you are involved in important alliance actions.

You usually **cannot leave** if:

- you are the Leader and have not transferred leadership
- you are leading an active rally
- you are participating in an active rally
- you have troops stationed in the Palace
- you still have troops committed to alliance-related actions

Before leaving, make sure your troops have returned and that you are no longer involved in any alliance operation.

---

## Being Removed from an Alliance

Leaders and Officers may remove members depending on rank permissions.

If a player is removed while they are not involved in active alliance actions, the removal happens normally.

If a player still has troops tied to an alliance action, the removal may be delayed until those troops return or the action ends.

This prevents broken states and keeps alliance warfare fair.

---

## Disbanding an Alliance

An alliance cannot be disbanded while it is still involved in important alliance activity.

An alliance usually **cannot be disbanded** if:

- it controls the Palace
- it has an active rally
- it still has troops committed to alliance structures or alliance objectives

Before disbanding, all critical alliance activity must be fully resolved.

---

## Important Restrictions

To keep alliance warfare fair and consistent, some actions are blocked while troops are committed elsewhere.

### You cannot:
- leave an alliance while your troops are in an active rally
- leave an alliance while your troops are stationed in the Palace
- disband an alliance while it controls the Palace
- leave as Leader without transferring leadership first

These restrictions are designed to prevent abuse and avoid broken troop states during important alliance actions.

---

## Tips for Players

### Join an active alliance early
Even a mid-sized active alliance is much better than staying alone.

### Watch your troop commitments
Before leaving an alliance, always check whether your troops are still tied to a rally or Palace defense.

### Respect alliance roles
Leaders and Officers manage strategy. Following alliance coordination is important during wars and events.

### Do not switch alliances casually during major events
Join cooldowns and troop restrictions can prevent you from moving freely when kingdom objectives are active.
