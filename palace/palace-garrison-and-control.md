# 🏰 Palace Garrison and Control

## Overview
After an alliance captures the Palace, it must defend it.

This is done through Palace control and defensive troop presence inside the Palace.

Holding the Palace is not only about taking it once. A strong alliance must also protect it from future attacks.

---

## Palace Control
Only one alliance can control the Palace at a time.

When an alliance controls the Palace:

- it becomes the current Palace owner
- it gains the benefits tied to Palace ownership
- it becomes the alliance responsible for Palace defense

If another alliance defeats the current owner, control changes immediately.

---

## Palace Garrison
The Palace can be defended by troops sent by members of the owning alliance.

This defensive force is the Palace garrison.

The garrison helps the alliance:

- hold control after capture
- resist incoming attacks
- prepare for repeated Palace battles

---

## Who Can Reinforce the Palace
Only members of the alliance that currently owns the Palace can send troops into its defense.

Players outside the owning alliance cannot reinforce the Palace.

---

## Recalling Palace Troops
A player may recall Palace troops only when Palace conditions allow it.

In general:

- troops may be recalled while the Palace is stable
- troops cannot be recalled during active Palace combat
- troop movements must not break Palace battle resolution

This prevents defensive abuse during live combat.

---

## Losing Palace Control
If the owning alliance is defeated:

- Palace ownership changes
- the old alliance loses its Palace benefits
- the new alliance becomes the current Palace owner
- Palace defense resets around the new controlling alliance

Because of this, alliances must always be ready to defend immediately after capture.

---

## Why Defense Matters
Many alliances can capture objectives once. Fewer alliances can keep them.

A Palace owner must:

- continue reinforcing defense
- organize members after capture
- prepare for counterattacks
- manage troop commitment carefully

Strong Palace defense is often what separates temporary control from real dominance.

---

## Tips for Players

### Do not leave key troops unavailable
If your alliance controls the Palace, troop availability matters for defense.

### Recall wisely
Withdrawing troops at the wrong moment may weaken Palace defense.

### Expect repeated attacks
Palace control often attracts immediate pressure from rival alliances.
